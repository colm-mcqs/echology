Equipment

We use the latest state of the art high resolution echosounder namely the Biosonics DTX Split Beam Echosounder.

The equipment can be mounted on a pole over the size of virtually any vessel and is portable enough to enable surveying in a range of environments.

Why Split Beam?

- With a split beam echosounder the acoustic pulse is split into four quadrants.

This enables the aspect of a fish to be determined which is important as this has a bearing on the amount of acoustic energy that it reflects. The use of a split beam echosounder is essential in order to determine fish biomass.

Data are processed using the specialist software SONAR 5 Pro, developed by Helge Baulk from the University of Oslo.
